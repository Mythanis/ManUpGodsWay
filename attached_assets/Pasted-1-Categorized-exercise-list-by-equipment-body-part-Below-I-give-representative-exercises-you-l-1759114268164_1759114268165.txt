2) 30-day whole-body workout plans (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced)

General notes before the plans:

30 days = 4 full weeks + 2 days (I lay out a 4-week scheme and map days across 30 calendar days).

Warm up 5–10 minutes before each workout (dynamic mobility, light cardio). Cool down + stretching 5–10 minutes after.

Rest between sets: Beginner 60–90s, Intermediate 45–75s, Advanced 30–60s (noted per plan).

Progression: increase weight or reps week-to-week or do an extra set on week 3–4.

If you lack a piece of equipment substitute with a bodyweight or dumbbell alternative from the categories above.

Plan structure summary

Beginner: 3 full-body workouts per week (Mon/Wed/Fri), active recovery or light cardio other days. Focus on learning movement, moderate volume.

Intermediate: 4 workouts per week (Upper/Lower/Full/Conditioning) — more volume and intensity.

Advanced: 5 workouts per week — split into push/pull/legs/full/conditioning with higher sets and some interval cardio.

A) Beginner — 30-day plan (3× week full-body — weeks 1–4)

Frequency: Week pattern Mon / Wed / Fri = Workout A / B / C repeated. Two light active recovery days (walk, mobility) and one rest day.

Workout A (Push + Legs + Core)

Bodyweight squat — 3 sets × 10–12 reps (tempo: 2s down / 1s up) — rest 60–90s

Incline push-ups (hands elevated to reduce load) — 3 × 8–12 — rest 60–90s

Dumbbell Romanian deadlift (light) — 3 × 10 — rest 60–90s

Dumbbell shoulder press (seated or standing) — 3 × 8–10 — rest 60–90s

Plank — 3 × 20–40 sec — rest 45–60s

Workout B (Pull + Legs + Core)

Assisted chin-up / negative chin-up or inverted row — 3 × 6–10 — rest 60–90s

Goblet squat (DB) — 3 × 10–12 — rest 60–90s

Dumbbell single-arm row — 3 × 8–10 per side — rest 60–90s

Glute bridge — 3 × 12–15 — rest 45–60s

Dead bug — 3 × 8–12 per side (controlled) — rest 45–60s

Workout C (Full body + conditioning)

Dumbbell bench press (flat) — 3 × 8–10 — rest 60–90s

Dumbbell reverse lunge — 3 × 8–10 per leg — rest 60–90s

KB or DB swing or light medicine-ball squat-to-press — 3 × 12–15 — rest 60s

Standing DB curl — 2 × 10–12 — rest 45–60s

Farmer carry (DB) — 3 × 30–60 seconds (distance) — rest 60s

Light cardio finisher: brisk walk or easy bike 12–20 minutes (steady)

Weekly progression (beginner): Weeks 1–2 focus on form; weeks 3–4 add 1 set to big lifts or +1–2 reps per set.

B) Intermediate — 30-day plan (4× week split)

Frequency: Mon (Upper A), Tue (Lower A), Thu (Upper B), Fri (Lower B). Weekend active rest / optional conditioning.

Upper A (Push emphasis)

Barbell or DB bench press — 4 × 6–8 — rest 60–90s

Seated DB shoulder press — 3 × 8–10 — rest 60s

Incline dumbbell fly or cable fly — 3 × 10–12 — rest 60s

Triceps rope pushdown (or DB overhead extension) — 3 × 10–12 — rest 45–60s

Plank with shoulder taps — 3 × 30–45s — rest 45s

Lower A (Heavy)

Back squat (barbell) or goblet if no bar — 4 × 6–8 — rest 90–120s

Romanian deadlift (barbell or DB) — 3 × 8–10 — rest 90s

Bulgarian split squat — 3 × 8–10 per leg — rest 60–90s

Standing calf raises — 3 × 12–15 — rest 45–60s

Upper B (Pull emphasis)

Pull-ups or Lat pulldown — 4 × 6–8 — rest 60–90s

Barbell bent-over row or single-arm DB row — 4 × 6–8 — rest 60–90s

Face pulls or rear delt fly (cable/DB) — 3 × 12–15 — rest 45–60s

Biceps curl (barbell or DB) — 3 × 10–12 — rest 45–60s

Lower B (Hypertrophy & conditioning)

Deadlift (Romanian or conventional) — 3 × 5–6 (if conventional) or 4 × 6–8 (RDL) — rest 90–120s

Leg press or front squat — 3 × 10–12 — rest 60–90s

Walking lunges — 3 × 12–16 steps total — rest 60s

Core circuit (Russian twists 30s + plank 30s) × 3 rounds — rest 60s between rounds

Weekly progression (intermediate): Add weight when you can complete top of rep range with good form; alternate a heavier week with slightly reduced volume every 4th week.

C) Advanced — 30-day plan (5× week split)

Frequency: Push / Pull / Legs / Full / Conditioning or accessory. Higher volume and intensity, includes variations, tempo and short intervals.

Day 1 — Push (heavy)

Barbell bench press — 5 × 5 — rest 90s

Overhead press (barbell) — 4 × 5–6 — rest 90s

Incline DB press — 3 × 8–10 — rest 60s

Weighted dips or triceps heavy — 3 × 6–8 — rest 60–90s

Lateral raise supersets: 3 × 12 + 3 × 15 (rear delt) — rest 45s

Day 2 — Pull (heavy)

Deadlift or rack pulls — 4 × 3–5 — rest 120s

Weighted pull-ups — 4 × 6–8 — rest 90s

Barbell row (Pendlay or bent) — 4 × 6–8 — rest 60–90s

Hammer curls / incline curls — 3 × 8–10 — rest 45–60s

Face pulls 3 × 15 — rest 45s

Day 3 — Legs (heavy + plyo)

Back squat — 5 × 5 — rest 120s

Front squat or Bulgarian split — 4 × 6–8 — rest 90s

Romanian deadlift heavy — 4 × 6–8 — rest 90s

Plyo finisher: Box jumps 3 × 6–8 (or jump lunges 3×10) — rest 60s

Calf heavy sets 4 × 10–15 — rest 45–60s

Day 4 — Full body / Hypertrophy

Incline DB press — 4 × 8–10 — rest 60s

Single-arm DB row — 4 × 8–10 — rest 60s

KB swings — 3 × 15 — rest 45–60s

Walking lunges — 3 × 12 per leg — rest 60s

Core superset: Hanging leg raises 3 × 12 + Pallof press 3 × 12 per side — rest 45–60s

Day 5 — Conditioning / Weakness work

HIIT bike or rowing intervals: 10–12 rounds of 20s all-out / 40s easy (total ~12–18 min)

Accessory: rotator cuff work, mobility, and band pull-aparts 3 × 15

Sets / reps / rest (advanced): Shorter rest on auxiliary lifts, heavier loads on compound lifts. Track intensity % if possible.

5) Safety, scaling, and final suggestions

If you’re new to resistance training, run the Beginner plan for 6–8 weeks before moving to Intermediate.

Advanced lifters should program deload weeks every 4–8 weeks.

If you have injuries or medical issues, run the plan by a clinician or physical therapist.
