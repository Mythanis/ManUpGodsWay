Palm Sunday Sermon: “The King You Didn’t Expect”

Text: John 12:12–19 (LSB)

Introduction: The Moment Everyone Thought They Understood

There are moments in life when people think they understand what is happening, but they completely miss what is actually going on.

Palm Sunday is one of those moments.

The crowd is loud.
The energy is high.
The expectations are rising.

Jesus is finally coming into Jerusalem, and it feels like everything is about to change.

They are waving palm branches.
They are shouting praise.
They are declaring Him King.

But here is the tension of Palm Sunday.

They were right about who He was, but wrong about what He came to do.

And if we are honest, we can do the same thing.

We can praise Jesus and still misunderstand Him.
We can celebrate Him and still try to shape Him into what we want Him to be.

Palm Sunday forces us to ask one question:

Do you want the real Jesus, or just the version of Him that fits your expectations?

1. The King Arrives with Purpose, Not Popularity

John 12:12–13 (LSB)
“On the next day, the large crowd who had come to the feast, when they heard that Jesus was coming to Jerusalem, took the branches of the palm trees and went out to meet Him, and were crying out, ‘Hosanna! Blessed is He who comes in the name of the Lord, even the King of Israel.’”

This is a massive moment.

The crowd is not quiet. They are crying out.
“Hosanna” means “save us now.”

They are declaring Jesus as King.

Palm branches were a national symbol. This was not just worship. This was political.
They were saying, “Here is our deliverer. Here is the one who will overthrow Rome.”

They wanted a king who would fix their external problems.

But Jesus did not come to meet their expectations.
He came to fulfill God’s purpose.

And here is where we need to pause.

Many people still approach Jesus this way.

Save my situation.
Fix my circumstances.
Make my life easier.

But Jesus did not come primarily to make your life comfortable.
He came to make you new.

He did not come to remove every struggle.
He came to deal with your sin.

Palm Sunday reminds us that Jesus is not a tool to serve our agenda.
He is the King we submit to.

2. The King Comes in Humility, Not Force

John 12:14–15 (LSB)
“Jesus, finding a young donkey, sat on it; as it is written, ‘Fear not, daughter of Zion; behold, your King is coming, seated on a donkey’s colt.’”

This is not how kings enter cities.

Kings ride war horses.
Kings come with armies.
Kings come with power and intimidation.

But Jesus comes on a donkey.

Why?

Because He is not coming to conquer Rome.
He is coming to conquer sin.

This is a fulfillment of Zechariah 9:9.

He is a different kind of King.

A humble King.
A righteous King.
A saving King.

The crowd expected a warrior.
God sent a Savior.

And this is where many people stumble.

They want a powerful Jesus who dominates their enemies.
But they struggle with a humble Jesus who calls them to surrender.

Philippians 2 tells us that Christ humbled Himself, even to the point of death on a cross.

If you want to follow Jesus, you do not just receive His salvation.
You follow His example.

This King does not just rule over you.
He calls you to die to yourself.

3. The Crowd Praised Him Without Understanding Him

John 12:16 (LSB)
“These things His disciples did not understand at the first; but when Jesus was glorified, then they remembered that these things were written of Him, and that they had done these things to Him.”

Even the disciples did not fully understand what was happening.

The crowd definitely did not.

They were celebrating a King they did not fully know.

And within days, many of these same voices would fall silent.
Some would even turn against Him.

This is the danger of shallow praise.

It is easy to praise Jesus when things look good.
It is easy to celebrate Him when He seems to be doing what you want.

But what happens when He does not meet your expectations?

What happens when following Him costs you something?

Palm Sunday teaches us that emotional excitement is not the same as genuine faith.

Real faith is not just loud on Sunday.
It is faithful on Friday.

4. The Real Mission Was Hidden in Plain Sight

John 12:17–19 (LSB)
“So the crowd who was with Him when He called Lazarus out of the tomb and raised him from the dead continued to bear witness. For this reason also the crowd went and met Him, because they heard that He had done this sign. So the Pharisees said to one another, ‘You see that you are not doing any good; look, the world has gone after Him.’”

The crowd followed Him because of miracles.

They saw power.
They saw signs.
They saw what He could do.

But they still missed why He came.

They thought the greatest miracle was raising Lazarus.
They had no idea the greater miracle was coming.

In just a few days:

Jesus would be betrayed.
Jesus would be beaten.
Jesus would be crucified.

And it would look like everything had gone wrong.

But in reality, everything was going exactly according to plan.

Because the King did not come to take a throne.
He came to take a cross.

The victory they wanted was temporary.
The victory He brought was eternal.

5. The Question Palm Sunday Leaves Us With

Palm Sunday is not just a historical moment.
It is a personal confrontation.

The same question the crowd faced is the same question you face today:

What kind of King do you want?

Do you want a King who serves your desires?
Or a King you surrender your life to?

Do you want a Jesus who fixes your circumstances?
Or a Savior who transforms your heart?

Do you want the crown without the cross?
Or are you willing to follow Him all the way?

Because you cannot separate Palm Sunday from Good Friday.

The same Jesus who was praised as King
is the same Jesus who was crucified as Savior.

Conclusion: From Palm Branches to the Cross

The crowd laid down palm branches.

But Jesus came to lay down His life.

They shouted, “Hosanna, save us now.”

And He answered that prayer in a way they never expected.

Not by overthrowing Rome.
But by defeating sin, death, and hell.

And here is the truth.

If you only see Jesus as a King who meets your expectations, you will eventually walk away.

But if you see Him as the Savior who died in your place, you will surrender your life.

Call to Response

Today, you have a choice.

You can be like the crowd
excited, emotional, but shallow.

Or you can truly follow Him.

Not just when it is easy.
Not just when it feels good.

But all the way.

Jesus is not just entering Jerusalem.

He is confronting your heart.

Will you crown Him as King,
or will you walk away when the cross comes?

Closing Prayer

Lord, we thank You for sending Your Son, not just as a King to admire, but as a Savior to surrender to. Forgive us for the times we have shaped You into our own expectations instead of submitting to who You truly are. Help us not to be like the crowd, but to be faithful followers who trust You, even when we do not understand. Teach us to lay down our lives just as You laid down Yours. We praise You as King, and we surrender to You as Lord. In Jesus’ name, Amen.
